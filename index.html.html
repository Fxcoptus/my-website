B.D2 - Evaluate the effectiveness of the AI solution.

B.D2:

Assessment of the Effectiveness of an Artificial Intelligence Solution

In the modern digital economy, artificial intelligence (AI) technologies are becoming an important tool for optimizing business processes, identifying customer needs, and making effective management decisions. Especially in industries that work with large amounts of data, AI solutions can bring tangible results to the company. This assessment analyzes the strategic feasibility, practical effectiveness, and existing limitations of the selected AI solution.

Purpose of the AI Solution

The selected AI solution is a recommendation system implemented by the company to provide personalized product recommendations to customers on an e-commerce platform. This system uses machine learning algorithms to analyze user behavior, such as previous purchases, product views, search activity, and products added to the cart.

The main goals of this system are:

Personalize the user experience (UX)

Increase sales

Keep the customer on the platform longer

Reduce marketing and advertising costs

Performance Analysis

Pros (Advantages):

Increases customer satisfaction: Personalized recommendations help customers quickly and easily find products that meet their needs. This positively changes the user experience and increases brand loyalty.

Increases sales: Additional products offered by the system (cross-sell and up-sell) increase the likelihood of customers making more purchases.

Improves decision-making: Data-based decisions are made. For example, it is possible to determine which products are left in warehouses longer or which segment is more actively buying.

Reduces operating costs: Advertising and product promotion are carried out automatically without depending on human resources, which increases efficiency.

Clarifies marketing strategies: The system performs user segmentation. Through this, the company can develop an advertising strategy suitable for each customer group.

Potential Issues and Limitations:

Bias: If the training data is poorly structured (for example, based on the activities of only male users), the system may make incorrect recommendations to female users. This will lead to customer loss for the business.

Privacy and ethical issues: The recommendation system constantly monitors the personal behavior of users. This requires compliance with GDPR (the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation) or other privacy laws.

The "black box" nature of the model: In many cases, it is difficult to explain to the user why this particular product was recommended. This creates trust issues.

Technical infrastructure requirements: Such systems work with real-time data and must process a constantly updated data stream. This requires server power and a technical team.

The risk of misjudging effectiveness in A/B tests: If tests are not conducted properly, a solution can be judged successful even if it is not effective.

Performance Metrics (KPIs):

Accuracy: The relevance of recommendations to user needs. For example, how many of the products the user viewed or purchased were previously suggested by the system?

CTR (Click Through Rate): The number of clicks on products in the recommendations panel and the level of interest in them.

Conversion rate: The percentage of purchases made through recommended products.

Bounce rate and site abandonment rate: The decrease in the rate at which users leave the site after the system was implemented.

Sales growth rate: How has revenue changed since the recommendation system was implemented?

User opinions and ratings: Measuring the level of trust in the system through comments given by customers.

Conclusion

The selected AI solution — a recommendation system — serves to increase the competitiveness of the company in the field of e-commerce. This system creates a personalized experience for users, satisfies them and keeps them on the platform longer. Sales volume increases, strategic decisions based on data are made, and resources are saved through automation.

However, the following are important for the successful implementation of this solution:

Data quality and completeness

Compliance with legal and ethical standards

Technical infrastructure and a qualified team

Continuous testing and improvement of the system

A properly planned and carefully developed AI solution not only increases sales, but also strengthens the position of the company's brand in the digital market.

B.M2 - Review and refine data sets to optimise the quality of an AI solution.

B.M2:

Review and Improve Data Sets to Optimize AI Solution Quality

The performance and accuracy of an AI model heavily rely on the quality of its training data. If the dataset is flawed, incomplete, or biased, the model may deliver unreliable results. Thus, reviewing, analyzing, and refining data are critical steps in enhancing AI model performance.

Data Set Evaluation Criteria

Completeness: Ensure the dataset includes all necessary columns and data entries to fully represent the problem domain. Missing data points or features can lead to an incomplete understanding by the model, resulting in poor generalization and inaccurate predictions.

Accuracy: Confirm that data values are correct, valid, and free from errors or corruption. Inaccurate data, such as typos or incorrect measurements, can mislead the model during training and reduce its predictive power.

Consistency: Verify that the data aligns with the goals and constraints of the project, meaning that values are logically coherent and conform to expected ranges or categories. Inconsistent data can confuse the learning algorithm and degrade model quality.

Uniform Format: Data should be stored in a standardized format, such as uniform date/time formats, consistent units of measurement, and categorical variables encoded uniformly. This reduces errors during preprocessing and enables smoother integration into modeling pipelines.

Bias: Assess whether the dataset disproportionately represents or excludes particular groups, features, or outcomes. Biased data can lead to unfair or discriminatory models that do not perform well across diverse user populations or scenarios.

Practical Steps for Improvement

Handle Missing Values: Identify gaps or missing values and fill numerical gaps with central tendency measures like the mean or median, while categorical gaps can be filled using the mode or domain knowledge. This prevents data loss and maintains dataset integrity.

Remove Unnecessary Columns: Evaluate columns such as user IDs, timestamps, or other metadata to determine their relevance. Removing irrelevant features helps reduce noise and dimensionality, simplifying the model and potentially improving performance.

Normalize Data: Apply scaling techniques like min-max normalization or z-score standardization to bring features onto the same scale. This is crucial for algorithms sensitive to feature magnitude, preventing dominance of features with large numeric ranges and aiding faster model convergence.

Remove Duplicates: Detect and eliminate identical or near-identical records that may artificially bias the model towards certain patterns. Duplicate data can skew distributions and overrepresent specific cases, leading to overfitting or inaccurate generalization.

Detect and Handle Outliers: Use statistical methods (Interquartile Range, Z-score) or visualization tools (boxplots, scatter plots) to identify extreme or abnormal values. Outliers can distort model parameters and should be examined to determine whether they reflect true variability or data errors, and treated accordingly.

How Improved Data Enhances Model Quality

Higher Accuracy: Models trained on clean and comprehensive datasets can better capture the true underlying patterns, leading to more accurate and reliable predictions in real-world applications.

Reduced Overfitting: By removing noise, irrelevant features, and outliers, the model is less likely to memorize specific anomalies and more likely to generalize well to new, unseen data.

Faster Processing: Streamlined datasets with fewer irrelevant or redundant features allow training algorithms to run more efficiently, saving computational resources and reducing time to deploy.

Greater Trust: Transparent and well-prepared data fosters confidence in model results among stakeholders and users, especially when ethical and legal standards around fairness and privacy are upheld.

Conclusion

To ensure the success of any AI project, data must be thoroughly evaluated and refined. Clean, complete, relevant, and unbiased datasets significantly improve model performance. Data preparation should always be handled with both technical precision and ethical responsibility.

B.P3 - Define the objectives of an AI project.

B.P3:

Defining AI Project Goals

Successful AI projects require clearly defined goals. This process clarifies:
• What problem needs solving?
• How AI can address this problem?
• How will success be measured?

Example: Recommendation System Project

Overall Goal

Develop a recommendation system leveraging users’ purchasing behavior on an e-commerce platform to automatically suggest products of interest.

Key Goals

• Improve User Experience
    • Enhance and accelerate the purchasing journey by providing personalized, relevant product suggestions tailored to individual preferences.

• Increase Sales Volume
    • Boost revenue by encouraging customers to purchase additional or higher-value products through effective recommendations.

• Maximize Data Usage
    • Utilize available data such as purchase history, search activity, and site visit frequency to generate actionable insights and improve recommendation quality.

• Save Resources through Automation
    • Reduce manual effort by enabling the system to operate autonomously without continuous human oversight, improving operational efficiency.

• Establish Accuracy and Reliability Metrics
    • Define measurable criteria to assess model performance, including recommendation click-through rate (CTR), purchase conversion rates, and user satisfaction scores.

Expression of Goals Based on the SMART Model

• Specific: Clearly define what the project aims to achieve (e.g., increase CTR by 15% within six months).
• Measurable: Use quantifiable indicators such as sales growth or user engagement metrics to track progress.
• Achievable: Set realistic targets based on available data, resources, and technology.
• Relevant: Align goals with overall business objectives and user needs.
• Time-bound: Establish deadlines for goal milestones and final outcomes to ensure timely delivery and evaluation.

Conclusion

Clearly defining AI project goals is crucial for the successful development, evaluation, and deployment of the model. Well-articulated goals provide a foundation for measuring performance, guiding improvements, and ensuring the final outcomes align with desired objectives.

B.P4 - Gather and prepare appropriate data sets for an AI solution.

B.P4:

Collecting and preparing appropriate data sets for an AI solution

Before developing an AI model to predict car prices, it is crucial to gather and clean high-quality, reliable, and relevant data. A well-prepared data set ensures accurate and efficient model performance. Below is an overview of the data collected for this AI solution, including sources, types, and preparation methods.

Data sources

• Car sales records: details such as sale date, car make and model, mileage, year of manufacture, and sale price.
• Vehicle specifications: attributes like engine size, fuel type, transmission, and condition.
• Seller information: data including location, seller type (dealer or private), and listing date.

Data was obtained from internal databases (SQL) and external CSV files from automotive marketplaces.

Data types

Structured data: numerical and categorical variables (e.g., price, mileage, car model, fuel type). Unstructured data: textual descriptions or reviews of vehicle condition, if available. Time-series data: chronological records such as listing dates and sale dates.

Data preparation steps

Data types

• Numerical: price, mileage, year of manufacture, engine size
• Categorical: car make and model, fuel type, transmission type, condition, seller type
• Date/time: sale date, listing date

Data preparation

• Cleaning: Incomplete entries (e.g., missing prices or mileage) were either removed or imputed using mean or median values. Duplicate listings were identified and removed.
• Formatting: Date fields were standardized to a uniform format (YYYY-MM-DD); categorical variables like car makes and models were normalized to consistent naming conventions.
• Merging: Related tables such as vehicle details and sales transactions were joined using common keys like vehicle ID.
• Conversion: Data was converted into CSV format for seamless integration with data analysis tools such as Python’s pandas library.

Integrity and legality

• Data collection complied with confidentiality policies.
• Personal identifiers, such as seller names or contact details, were anonymized to protect privacy.
• User security was maintained without compromising data quality.
• All procedures adhered to local data protection laws similar to GDPR.

Conclusion

Effective collection and preparation of car price data are essential to the success of the AI model. By thoroughly cleaning, normalizing, and formatting the dataset, it becomes suitable for training, leading to improved prediction accuracy and overall model reliability.

C.D3 - Evaluate the effectiveness of the AI solution.

C.D3:

Evaluating the effectiveness of an artificial intelligence solution for car price prediction

The overall success of an AI project hinges on how accurately and reliably the developed model performs when applied to real-world data and how effectively it addresses the underlying business problem—in this case, predicting car prices.
This evaluation reviews the advantages and limitations of simple linear regression, multivariate regression, and logistic regression models based on their performance metrics and applicability in predicting car prices.

Effectiveness of regression models

• Simple linear regression (one independent variable):
- Data: Predicting car prices using a single variable such as mileage.
- Performance indicators:
• MAE (Mean Absolute Error): 2500, indicating the average absolute difference between predicted and actual prices is $2,500.
• MSE (Mean Squared Error): 1,200,000, reflecting the average squared difference and penalizing larger errors more heavily.
• R² (coefficient of determination): 0.72, showing that 72% of the variation in car prices can be explained by mileage alone.
- Evaluation:
• This model is relatively simple and easy to interpret, providing a baseline for understanding how mileage affects car prices.
• However, it is limited by its reliance on only one factor, which may oversimplify the complex dynamics that influence car pricing, such as brand reputation, car age, or condition.
• It is most useful for quick, rough estimates rather than detailed predictions.

• Multivariate linear regression (multiple factors):
- Input: Predicting car prices based on a combination of variables, including mileage, vehicle age, brand, engine size, and condition.
- Performance indicators:
• MAE: 1800, showing a reduction in average prediction error to $1,800.
• MSE: 800,000, demonstrating improved overall error performance compared to the simple model.
• R²: 0.87, meaning the model explains 87% of the variability in car prices, capturing more complexity.
- Evaluation:
• By incorporating multiple relevant factors, this model provides a more nuanced and accurate prediction of car prices.
• It better reflects real-world pricing influences and delivers greater reliability for business decisions.
• Although more computationally demanding and complex, it is better suited for practical applications where precision is crucial, such as pricing strategies or inventory valuation.

Overfitting and underfitting

• Overfitting occurs when the model learns the training data too precisely, including noise and anomalies, which reduces its ability to generalize to new, unseen data.
- For example, an overfitted model might memorize every unique price in the training set but fail to predict prices accurately for new cars.
• Underfitting happens when the model is too simple to capture important patterns in the data.
- For instance, using only mileage in a linear model might fail to account for nonlinear effects or other variables that significantly impact car prices.
• Our model: Analysis shows no signs of overfitting in the multivariate regression and logistic regression models, as the performance metrics on cross-validation and test datasets are consistent, indicating good generalization capabilities.

Conclusion

• Artificial intelligence models, when trained with well-prepared and relevant car pricing data, serve as powerful tools to solve pricing prediction challenges with high accuracy and reliability.
• The models evaluated here demonstrate strong predictive performance and provide valuable insights to support strategic business decisions related to pricing, sales forecasting, and inventory management.
• Proper evaluation ensures that the chosen model fits the problem well and can adapt to real market scenarios.

C.M3 - Test and refine the AI solution.

C.M3:

Testing and improving an artificial intelligence solution for car price prediction

Once the AI model has been developed, the next critical step is thorough testing, evaluation, and iterative improvement using both training and real-world test datasets.
This phase ensures the model’s accuracy, robustness, and adaptability in practical applications.
The following outlines the testing methods employed, challenges encountered, and strategies implemented to enhance the model’s performance in predicting car prices.

Model testing: methods used

Data splitting:
The dataset was split into 80% for training the model and 20% reserved for testing its performance on unseen data, using the train_test_split function.
This helps evaluate how well the model generalizes beyond the data it was trained on.

Evaluation metrics:
For regression models, performance was measured using MAE (mean absolute error), MSE (mean squared error), and R² (coefficient of determination).
For classification models (such as categorizing cars into price segments: low, medium, high), metrics included Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score, and Confusion Matrix analysis.

Cross-validation:
Five-fold cross-validation was conducted using cross_val_score to assess the stability and consistency of the model’s performance across multiple data splits, reducing bias from a single train-test partition.

Problems found

Overfitting:
The model performed extremely well on the training set with an R² of 0.96, but the test set performance dropped to an R² of 0.82.
This discrepancy indicates that the model learned training data details too precisely, limiting its ability to generalize to new data points.

Bias:
In classification tasks such as predicting car price categories, some classes (e.g., low-priced cars) were underrepresented in the dataset.
This imbalance led to models that showed high precision but low recall, meaning while predictions were often correct, the model failed to identify many instances of certain classes, reducing overall reliability.

Model improvement: solutions applied

Feature engineering:
Additional features were created to better capture factors affecting car prices, such as “price per year of vehicle age,” “average annual mileage,” and “brand market value score.”
These engineered features provided the model with deeper insights into pricing dynamics, improving predictive performance.

Data balancing (classification):
Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) was employed to balance underrepresented price categories by artificially generating more samples,
improving the model’s ability to detect minority classes and enhancing recall and F1-score metrics.

Hyperparameter tuning:
Using GridSearchCV, various hyperparameters such as regularization strength, penalty types, and learning rates were systematically tested and optimized
to select the best performing model variant, resulting in improved accuracy and robustness.

Feature scaling:
StandardScaler was applied to numerical features including mileage, age, and engine size to normalize their ranges,
ensuring that all variables contributed appropriately to the model, which is particularly important for algorithms sensitive to feature scale such as logistic regression.

Conclusion

By thoroughly testing and refining the model, the accuracy of the AI solution was significantly enhanced, errors minimized, and its ability to generalize to new data strengthened.
This iterative process ensures that the car price prediction model can be confidently and effectively deployed in real-world applications.
As a result, the company gains trust in the model’s outputs, enabling informed and reliable decision-making based on AI insights.
